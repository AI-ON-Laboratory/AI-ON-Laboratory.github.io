# Explainable Multimodal Models for Predicting Immunotherapy Response in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

---

## Goal

Explainability is a clinical and ethical requirement in oncology - clinicians cannot trust what they cannot understand. Despite the promise of multimodal AI models for cancer prognosis, XAI methods for such architectures remain largely underdeveloped, representing a significant open gap in the literature.

The thesis aims to design, implement, and evaluate an XAI framework for multimodal fusion models integrating clinical/blood data, digital pathology images, and CT scans of NSCLC patients, with the clinical goal of supporting oncologists in patient stratification and treatment decision-making.

---

## Data

The thesis uses multimodal data from NSCLC patients: structured clinical and blood variables, digital pathology whole-slide images (WSI), and CT scan volumes.

---

## Project Description

The student will implement explainability methods for multimodal architectures: late fusion, combining unimodal model predictions, or intermediate fusion, merging feature representations via cross-attention or concatenation-based modules.

---

## Requirements

Mandatory: courses in Machine Learning and Deep Learning,. 

A plus: prior exposure to XAI methods (SHAP, LIME, Grad-CAM).

## Calibration of Large Language Models to assess the trustworthiness in cancer decision-making

---

## Goal

Evaluating how well LLM are calibrated in decision-making tasks and to analyze how calibration is influenced by factors such as model type, task type, and confidence estimation strategies. 

Investigating whether post-hoc calibration techniques can improve reliability.

---

## Data

Type: textual data

Source: Apollo 11 multicenter italian study for patients with lung cancer

---

## Clinical context:

LLMs are increasingly used in clinical decision-making but their reliability in healthcare remains uncertain. This study focuses on calibration, which measures whether a model’s confidence matches its actual correctness, since poorly calibrated and overconfident models may be risky in medical applications.

---

## Project Description

- Survey on the calibration techniques in LLM literature
- Implementation of calibration techniques
- Apply and evaluate on Apollo 11 data
